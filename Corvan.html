Corvan

"Rest assured that the gods are not asteemed anymore, my lord, now I can see you and I've seen it before. Shed no grace on me." -Corvan


Backstory

Corvan was an emperial soldier in Vertigonia during the The Dark Age. He was given the muatgens and training to become a witcher, but he ultimately set his sights on higher goals. Corvan was taken notice of by a particular few lesser deities at the very beginning of his life, including Yelvatana and Yelvata, the goddess and god of death. In addition to these he was granted a trial by the Lady of the Lake, and she gifted him with the legendary sword Arodonite. Using this sword, and also the legendary hammer Mjolnir, he fought a battle against a great dragon in Daleth. In the end Corvan sacrificed himself to kill the dragon, but found himself in the afterlife with much more power then he had first anticipated. Using these newfound strengths he led the mortal armies to rise up and take Godhome. And later the spirit armies of The Hall of Souls to cast down Azathoth, freeing the unrealities of half the universe.

Details

Corvan was angered by the tyranny of the gods and so he sparked the The Radiant War. This crusade lasted for over a thousand years as he crushed pantheon after pantheon, casting the souls of those slain gods down into other worlds. His companion Elwin Althorn and also his servant Thea assisted him in most every seige and battle he took part in, these two had perished by the end of The Ruinations while Corvan seemed to live on. See the Siege of Cinder for the first true death of Corvan. Later he was killed by a queen of the Phyrexians, a second true death.
