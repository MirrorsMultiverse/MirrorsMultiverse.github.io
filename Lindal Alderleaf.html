Lindal Alderleaf

Summary

Lindal Alderleaf was a member of the Thieves' Guild clan The Black Hand, the son of its leader Landil Shadefell. After being expelled from the clan at his father's death, he worked his way up in the world, became an avatar of The Watcher, and then reclaimed control of the Thieves' Guild from his half-sister, Lundil Shadefell. He eventually became a powerful king of the criminal underworld, taking his father's crown. He is a multiversal agent of chaos, a nuisance to the just, and a plague upon the righteous.

The Halving

At one point in his life, while in his father's mausoleum, at his father's grave, Lindal found a shimmering piece of cloth, which would come to be known as The Cloak of Landil. When he touched it, his soul was split and infinitely. When he touched it, his soul was split and infinitely duplicated by its wild magic, in a multiversal event known as The Halving of the Halfling. As a result, an incarnation of Lindal (and thusly The Watcher) exists in every timeline and nearly every universe. Lindal is always either the number one most wanted by the Gatewatch or on the side of the Gatewatch. He was one of the most powerful humanoids in existence, but he ultimately fell to the wayside under the reign of Corvan. Corvan has attempted to kill Lindal on multiple instances and succeeded almost every time, but with endless incarnations the Halfling Watcher is practically immortal. Lindal's threr most prominent incarnations dwell in Vertigonia, Vetras,Castfar.

Lindal of Vertigonia

The original Lindal. Born to his human father and his Halfling mother Saorcia Alderleaf, he was raised in the ways of the Thieves' Guild. At his father's mysterious passing, he was usurped by his half-sister Lundil Shadefell, and fled the clan, fearing for his life. Alone and on the road, he met the Human Primrose the Dancer and the Elf Elerrina Starborne, who journeyed with him for a time. They received a quest while in the fledgling city of Daleth, and there fought a dragon. Later, while the party was trying to save the child Elva, Lindal was marked by Yelvatana to become an avatar of The Watcher, along with Elva. The group followed Elva as her Watcher powers grew, and Lindal witnessed the Battle between Elerrina, her patron deity Narria, and Elva, which resulted in the Watcher fleeing. Shortly thereafter Lindal and his companions were captured by Lundil and The Black Hand. After seeing both of his companions killed, he was forced to fight Lundil in gladiatorial combat, and twice he saved himself from death by sheer force of will. Eventually he was presumed dead, and taken to the mausoleum of his father. There he found The Cloak of Landil, and not knowing of The Halving that had taken place, he used it to sneak around the clan's city, recovering from his fight making allies in the Black Hand. Eventually his Watcher powers developed to fruition, and he challenged his half-sister for the leadership of the clan. He defeated her, and proceeded to use his powers to expand the black hand, eventually going to the empire of Fasadonia, to its capital city of Fasar, and killing the evil king Fasadus and Elva. He then conquered vast expanses of Kanta (Vertigonia) in his new empire Lindonia. His conquests were only halted by his former companions Elerrina and Primrose, who had been sent by the gods from the afterlife to raise an army of Dragons to stop the Watcher. The massive battle that ensued, The Battle of Lindonia, would have gone on endlessly but for Lindal's choice to use his Watcher powers to turn all living things in the surrounding area, including himself, into stone. Thus ended the Vertigonian Lindal, unless some brave advenurer were to explore that haunted city of stone...

Lindal of Castfar

One variant of Lindal appeared in Castfar, in the city of Cosmeria. He was quickly recruited by the Gatewatch of that realm, but lived a mostly peaceful life, running a fish shop and occasionally checking in with the Gatewatch. This existence continued for 20 years, until by chance or fate he met Arimathea Oxicat and company, and joined a quest...
This version of Lindal has a flying octopus named Octavius.

Lindal of Vetras

Yet another variant of Lindal appeared in the City of Cinder in Vetras. This Lindal also joined the Gatewatch, and befriended the stable boy Capricorn. This Lindal was responsible for wounding Corvan during the Siege of Cinder, although he himself was killed in the process. His memory lives on in a friend who never forgot him, The Time Knight.
